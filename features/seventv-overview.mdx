[7TV](https://7tv.app) is an emote platform for Twitch and Kick that also offers a suite of subscriber cosmetics. Chatterino7 integrates these features natively, so you see paints, personal emotes, animated avatars, and badges exactly as other 7TV users do — without any browser extension.

## Connecting your 7TV account

Chatterino7 links your 7TV cosmetics automatically via your Twitch login. No separate 7TV login is required.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Go to **File → Settings → Accounts** (or press **Ctrl+P** and search for Accounts).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your Twitch account">
    Click **Add account** and log in with Twitch. Chatterino7 uses your Twitch identity to look up your 7TV profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cosmetics load automatically">
    Once logged in, your 7TV name paint, personal emote set, and animated avatar are fetched automatically. Other users' cosmetics are loaded as you open channels.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You need an active 7TV subscription to display name paints and personal emotes. Badges are awarded separately by 7TV. Animated avatars require you to have set a 7TV profile picture.
</Note>

## 7TV EventAPI — real-time updates

Chatterino7 maintains a persistent WebSocket connection to the **7TV EventAPI**. This connection handles live updates so you never need to restart or manually refresh to see cosmetic changes:

- When a channel editor adds or removes an emote from a channel emote set, the change appears instantly in chat.
- When a user updates their personal emote set, the new set is pushed to all connected clients.
- When 7TV assigns or revokes a paint or badge entitlement, the change is reflected immediately without restarting.

The client subscribes per-channel as you open splits, and unsubscribes when you close them, keeping the connection efficient. The EventAPI heartbeat interval is 25 seconds; Chatterino7 reconnects automatically if the connection drops.

## High-resolution and AVIF image support

Chatterino7 requests **4x image variants** from 7TV and FrankerFaceZ when they are available, so emotes look sharp on HiDPI and Retina displays.

In addition, Chatterino7 supports the **AVIF image format**. AVIF provides better compression and quality than WebP or PNG, particularly for animated emotes. When a 7TV emote offers an AVIF variant and your build includes AVIF support, Chatterino7 selects it automatically.

<Tip>
  If your Linux build does not include a static `libavif`, install `qavif.so` from [kimageformats](https://invent.kde.org/frameworks/kimageformats) and Chatterino7 will pick it up at runtime.
</Tip>

## Platform support

All 7TV cosmetics work on both **Twitch** and **Kick** channels. Paints, personal emotes, and badges are tracked per platform — a user's Twitch and Kick accounts are each resolved independently against their 7TV profile.
