title: "AutoMod"
description: "Review and act on AutoMod-held messages directly inside Chatterino7."
---

Chatterino7 integrates with Twitch's AutoMod system, letting moderators and broadcasters approve or reject held messages without leaving the client.

## The AutoMod channel

When AutoMod holds a message for review, it appears in a special virtual channel. Open a split pointed at `/automod` to see held messages in real time:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a new split">
    Press `Ctrl+T` to create a new split.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the channel name">
    Type the channel name of the channel you moderate (not `/automod` literally — enter the actual channel). AutoMod-held messages for a channel appear within that channel's AutoMod queue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the AutoMod highlight">
    In **Settings → Highlighting**, enable **Highlight AutoMod messages** to make held messages visually distinct. You can also enable a sound alert for new AutoMod messages.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
AutoMod-held messages are only visible to moderators and the broadcaster of the channel.
</Note>

## How AutoMod messages appear

Messages held by AutoMod are displayed in the channel's chat split with a distinct highlight (configured in Settings → Highlighting under the AutoMod section). The held message shows:

- The author's username and message content.
- The AutoMod reason or category (harassment, profanity, etc.) as provided by Twitch.
- **Approve** and **Deny** action buttons.

The `flags.automod` identifier in filter expressions evaluates to `true` for these messages, so you can use filters to create a split that shows only AutoMod-held content.

## Approving and rejecting messages

Click **Approve** to allow the message to appear in chat for all viewers. Click **Deny** to reject it. Both actions are sent immediately to the Twitch API.

<Tip>
Use a dedicated split with the filter `flags.automod` applied to keep AutoMod messages separate from regular chat, especially useful in high-volume channels.
</Tip>

## AutoMod highlights

You can configure AutoMod messages to trigger a highlight with a custom color, sound, and taskbar alert in **Settings → Highlighting**:

- Enable **Highlight AutoMod messages**.
- Enable **Play sound for AutoMod highlights** and optionally specify a custom sound file.
- Enable **Flash taskbar for AutoMod highlights** to get an OS-level notification.

AutoMod highlights do not appear in the Mentions tab by default.

## Low-trust users

Messages from users marked as low-trust by Twitch are flagged with `flags.restricted` and `flags.monitored` in filter expressions. You can use `/lowtrust` to open the low-trust user management panel, or use `/monitor <user>` and `/restrict <user>` from the command line.
